CAPSTONE · GRADING RUBRIC

Capstone Rubric

The capstone is worth 25% of your final grade. Each of the four dimensions below is weighted equally — 6.25% of the course grade per dimension. Here's exactly what each level looks like.

How to read this: Four dimensions, four levels each. Your grade on each dimension is the level that best describes your work. Final capstone grade = average of the four dimensions × 25.

1. Code Clarity (25%)

Can someone else read your code and understand what it does?

EXCELLENT · 4

Code is logically organized, well-commented at decision points, uses descriptive variable names. Every cleaning step has a one-line comment explaining the choice. No dead code. Runs top-to-bottom without errors. README explains how to run it in 3-5 lines.

GOOD · 3

Code runs cleanly, generally readable, most decisions are clear from context. Some comments missing where they'd help. Variable names mostly meaningful. README exists and is functional.

DEVELOPING · 2

Code runs but is hard to follow. Variable names like df2, x, thing. Cleaning steps not justified. Possibly some dead code or commented-out experiments. README minimal.

INCOMPLETE · 1

Code crashes, won't run end-to-end, or is so disorganized a reader cannot follow it. No README or unhelpful README.

2. Visualizations (25%)

Do your charts communicate, or do they just exist?

EXCELLENT · 4

Every chart has a clear title, labeled axes, and units. Chart type matches the question (bar for category comparison, line for time, scatter for two-variable relationship). Colors used intentionally. A reader gets the point without explanation. At least one chart per business question.

GOOD · 3

Charts have titles and labels, chart type is reasonable. Minor formatting issues (small text, awkward scale). Three charts present, one per question.

DEVELOPING · 2

Charts present but partly mislabeled or unlabeled. Default pandas/matplotlib styling left as-is. Wrong chart type for the question (pie chart for time series, etc.). Fewer than three charts.

INCOMPLETE · 1

No charts, or charts are unreadable, mislabeled to the point of being misleading, or are obvious copy-pastes that don't reflect the data.

3. Insight Quality (25%)

Are your three findings actually interesting and supported by the data?

EXCELLENT · 4

Three distinct, non-obvious findings. Each is stated as a specific number with context ("Furniture drove 34% of Q4 revenue, vs. 22% for Electronics — a reversal from Q3"). Findings connect to a plausible business action.

GOOD · 3

Three findings, each supported by the data. Numbers cited. Findings are accurate but somewhat surface-level — what most people would notice on a first look.

DEVELOPING · 2

Findings stated as opinions or vague ("Electronics sold well"). Numbers not cited or unclear. Two of the three findings are the same finding rephrased.

INCOMPLETE · 1

No findings, or findings not supported by the analysis shown, or findings contradicted by the data.

4. Communication (25%)

Written report and 5-minute presentation combined.

EXCELLENT · 4

Report is clear, scannable, and a non-technical reader could follow it. Presentation is rehearsed, hits the 5-minute mark, walks through one finding clearly with a chart. Speaker is comfortable taking a question.

GOOD · 3

Report is complete and readable. Presentation lands the main point. Minor issues with pacing or visuals on slides. Handles questions reasonably.

DEVELOPING · 2

Report present but rambling or unclear. Presentation runs short or long, reads from notes, or doesn't show a chart. Struggles with basic questions about own work.

INCOMPLETE · 1

No report, no presentation, or presentation that does not explain the work.

Calculation

Each dimension scored 1-4. Final capstone score = (sum of 4 dimensions) ÷ 16 × 100.

Example: 4 + 3 + 4 + 3 = 14. 14 ÷ 16 × 100 = 87.5%. That capstone score × 25% = 21.9% of your final grade.

Disclosure penalty

Undisclosed AI use, if discovered after submission, drops the final capstone score by one full level on every dimension. Don't risk it — disclose honestly per the AI Policy.
