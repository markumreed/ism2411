WEEK 16 · CAPSTONE PROJECT

Capstone — Retail Sales Analysis

A single sustained project using a real-shaped retail sales dataset of about 5,000 rows. You will clean it, ask three business questions, answer them with code and visualizations, and present your findings. This is the deliverable you'll add to your portfolio.

What this is: A practical demonstration that you can take a CSV file no one has explained to you, figure out what it contains, decide what's interesting, and tell that story clearly. This is the exact skill employers are paying for.

The dataset

You'll receive a file called retail_sales.csv at the start of Week 13. It contains roughly 5,000 rows of fictional retail transactions with these columns:

  • order_date — date of the sale (mostly clean, with some bad values)
  • product_category — one of about 8 categories (with some typos)
  • region — one of North, South, East, West (some nulls)
  • unit_price — dollar amount per item (some negatives = returns)
  • quantity_sold — units in the order (some zeros)
  • customer_segment — Consumer, Corporate, or Home Office

The data is intentionally messy. Cleaning it well is half the assignment.

Deliverables

  1. A single Python script or Jupyter notebook (capstone_yourname.py or .ipynb) that loads the raw CSV, cleans it, computes your analysis, and produces your visualizations.
  2. A short written report (1-2 pages, PDF) — the three business questions you chose, your findings, and one chart per question. This is what a manager would read.
  3. A 5-minute presentation in class during Week 16 — walk us through one finding, show one chart, tell us the business implication.
  4. A GitHub repo containing all of the above plus a README.md explaining how to run your code.

Example business questions

You choose three. Here are examples — pick from these or invent your own (better):

  • Which product category drove the most revenue, and was that consistent across regions?
  • Is there a seasonal pattern in sales? Which months are strongest?
  • How does the Corporate segment differ from Consumer in average order size?
  • Are there products that appear to be returns-heavy (high count of negative unit_price)?
  • Which region has the highest average order value, and what's driving that?
  • What share of revenue comes from the top 20% of customer segments by volume?

What "good" looks like

  • Your cleaning steps are explained — every dropna or fillna or astype has a comment saying why
  • Your charts have titles, axis labels, and reasonable formatting (not the default unlabeled mess)
  • Your three findings are stated as numbers with context — "Furniture drove 34% of revenue, vs. 22% for Electronics" not "Furniture sold a lot"
  • Your code runs from top to bottom without errors if someone clones your repo and runs it
  • Your README tells a reader how to run your code in 3-5 lines

What "weak" looks like

  • Unlabeled charts copy-pasted from default pandas plotting
  • Findings stated as opinions ("I think Electronics is popular")
  • Code that crashes halfway through because cleaning was incomplete
  • A README that just says "run the file"
  • Three "questions" that are actually one question rephrased
